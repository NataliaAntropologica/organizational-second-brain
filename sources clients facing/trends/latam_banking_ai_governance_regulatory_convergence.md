## Key Signal

The central banks of Brazil, Canada, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Peru, and the United States have collaborated on AI governance frameworks for financial services. The work addresses risk management, supervisory approaches, and governance structures for AI adoption in banking. Chile and Uruguay are identified as the most AI-ready jurisdictions in Latin America, with data availability as their primary challenge. Colombia and Peru show strengths in talent and governance but face challenges in private sector and civil society engagement.

## Why It Matters

Regulatory convergence on AI governance is a structural trend. When central banks collaborate across borders on AI risk frameworks, it signals that AI governance compliance will become a table-stakes requirement for financial institutions operating across multiple LatAm markets — precisely Banco Andino's profile (Colombia, Peru, Chile). The identification of data availability as the primary challenge in AI-ready jurisdictions reinforces H1: data architecture is the actual AI bottleneck, recognized now at the central bank level.

## Implication for Sintética

AI governance is becoming a strategic necessity, not a compliance checkbox. Banco Andino, operating in three jurisdictions with emerging AI regulatory frameworks, will need to navigate differentiated requirements in each market. Sintética's AI Strategy practice has an opportunity to develop a cross-jurisdictional AI governance advisory offering — combining strategic positioning with regulatory navigation. This is defensible territory that pure technology consultants cannot easily occupy. The data availability gap identified by BIS for Chile also validates the AI Readiness Diagnostic opportunity: clients will be asked about it by regulators.
