## Key Signal

Latin American banking is exiting the pilot phase of AI adoption and entering a phase of strategic-scale implementation. The shift is characterized by less emphasis on isolated proofs of concept and more on impact at the business level, customer protection, and scalable deployment with strategic intent. This transition requires sustained investment, continuous training, and regulatory frameworks adapted to the new landscape.

## Why It Matters

This is a structural shift, not a speed increase. Banks that only ran pilots are now being asked to commit to transformation roadmaps. The organizations that defined their AI strategy before selecting implementation partners will have significantly more control over outcomes. Those that did not will inherit their vendor's strategy.

## Implication for Sintética

The advisory window is now. Banks in this transition phase need a strategic layer before they commit to platforms and vendors. Sintética's AI Strategy practice (Decision 001) is directly positioned for this moment. Banco Andino, currently pursuing digital transformation without a defined AI strategy, fits this profile precisely. The risk: if Banco Andino selects an implementation partner without strategic guidance first, the engagement opportunity narrows to execution support.

## Weak Signals to Watch

- Banks announcing multi-year "AI transformation" partnerships with technology vendors (signals advisory window closing)
- Chief AI Officer or Chief Data Officer appointments at regional banks (signals internal strategy is forming without external advisors)
- Regulatory guidance on AI use cases in Colombia, Peru, Chile (signals compliance-driven adoption accelerating)
