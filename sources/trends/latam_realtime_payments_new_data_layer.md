## Key Signal

Peru, Chile, and Argentina are entering a new phase of real-time payments growth. In Peru, the central bank's push for real-time payments and interoperability could transform the payments ecosystem and significantly expand financial inclusion. In Chile, adoption is moving beyond person-to-person payments to merchant payments and everyday transactions. Colombia is projected to bring 5.1 million previously unbanked people into the financial system — the largest financial inclusion expansion in Latin America.

## Why It Matters

Real-time payments infrastructure is not just a payments story — it is a data story. Every real-time transaction generates a behavioral signal. Banks that can capture and interpret these signals in real time have a structural customer intelligence advantage over those that process data in batches. The shift to real-time payments in Banco Andino's three markets (Colombia, Peru, Chile) simultaneously creates a new data layer and a new competitive pressure.

## Implication for Sintética

Banco Andino operates in exactly the three markets where this infrastructure shift is accelerating. This means: (1) the customer data landscape is about to become richer and more real-time, which makes data fragmentation more costly to maintain; (2) new-to-bank customers in Colombia's inclusion expansion are digital-native from day one, with different data profiles and different AI personalization potential; (3) fintechs will build on this infrastructure faster than traditional banks. The AI Strategy engagement at Banco Andino should explicitly address real-time data as a strategic asset.

## Weak Signals to Watch

- Fintech players launching in Colombia/Peru/Chile targeting newly banked populations
- Banco Andino's competitors announcing real-time customer intelligence capabilities
- Regulatory mandates for payment data sharing (open banking) in these markets
